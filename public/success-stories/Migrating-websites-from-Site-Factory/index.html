Migrating Pfizer websites from Site Factory to Edison Lite 

Success Story: Migrating Pfizer websites from Site Factory to Edison Lite

by Platform DevOps

Published: Mar 30, 2021

When part of Pfizer’s external enterprise hosting platform was growing outdated, Platform DevOps had a better, cheaper, and more flexible solution at the ready.

Migrating Pfizer’s websites from Site Factory to Edison Lite reduced costs >$215,000 per year - read on to find out why and how.



Platform DevOps is a cross-disciplinary expert team responsible for developing digital platforms and services that streamline and strengthen Pfizer’s common digital processes. While many of the team’s projects arise from discussions with various parts of the business, they’re also tasked with proactively managing the digital lifecycle as well as anticipating potential problems and new opportunities surrounding Pfizer’s technology.

In early 2020, it became clear that one of the commonly-used enterprise hosting solutions needed replacement. This resulted in migrating hundreds of Pfizer websites to an approved, established, internal option: The Edison Lite offering.

A platform in need of replacement

For many years, Pfizer used Site Factory – a third party, cloud-based, enterprise software platform – to manage, build, and host part of its portfolio of websites. This was a category of website that was largely similar in functionality and required low levels of self-service. The Pfizer Digital team recognized that the technology behind Site Factory was growing outdated and would reach End of Life (EOL) - when a technology stops receiving maintenance and repairs - in the not-too-distant future.

In addition, while Site Factory worked well when business units had to create and clone a standard set of websites, the pharmaceutical industry landscape is rapidly changing and developing. Digital answers to those challenges have to come just as fast. New ideas and requirements were hard to implement within its framework and Site Factory websites would be very expensive to update.

Teams within Pfizer were looking for a cost-effective, easier, more flexible way to deploy these types of websites.

A Pfizer platform at the ready

The decision whether to adopt another external enterprise software toolset or go with an in-house solution was an easy one in the end. Pfizer Digital had already created Edison, a two-stream internal web publishing platform that was fully-supported, purpose-built and designed for pharma industry regulations and compliance.

Edison Lite, for simpler sites with limited interactivity, was a good fit for publishing the majority of existing Site Factory-based websites, while Edison Custom can be tailored to more complex needs. The Edison platform gives Pfizer Digital the ability to build and support any website or digital service cheaper and easier than before — and without relying on external solutions.

IN FOCUS

Project Santa Claus saw the full web infrastructure for supporting the Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine planned (and replanned many times as requirements changed and developed) and deployed in record time in 2020.

This rapid, crisis-response project would never have been possible without Edison Lite and its supporting internal platforms, like Canvas Web Builder and Stratus microservices.

500 websites to migrate: Not a “snap your fingers” job

The Pfizer Digital team was tasked with assessing roughly 500 digital properties for migration from Site Factory to Edison Lite. This was also a chance to (re)build many sites, tailoring them to Pfizer’s current business needs, and update the platform accordingly.

After conversations with Solution Center Owners — those responsible for their business’s website content and mission — the team narrowed down the number of websites to migrate to 276.

Even though around 200 could simply be retired, this still left them with a big job, migrating 300+ web properties to Edison Lite! Corporate sites were often straightforward to deal with, but those for Health Care Providers all required additional authentication layers. The teams had to navigate discussions with business and technical stakeholders until everyone was happy with the new solutions.

Streamlined processes for Solution Center Owners

Migrating hundreds of websites has helped streamline Pfizer Digital’s website retirement process, too. It’s now far easier for Solution Center Owners to approve a website’s retirement and let the Operations team handle the rest.

Pfizer’s business teams can now build simple sites quicker and easier than ever, with little-to-no technical knowledge in many site maintenance tasks. Self-service content editing and drag-and-drop tools like Canvas Web Builder make the experience a breeze. For more complicated sites, designers, developers, and technical teams are ready to help Solution Center Owners get the most out of Pfizer Digital’s custom tools.

Fully-migrated by mid-2021

The Pfizer Digital Team plans to continue migrating the remaining Site Factory corporate sites to Edison Lite through the end of June 2021. At that point, all sites previously on Site Factory will be hosted and managed internally.

Success metrics:

  • Site Factory websites migrated to Edison Lite: 111
  • Site Factory websites retired: 85
  • Site Factory websites pending to migrate to Edison Lite: 55
  • Total websites: 251
  • Annual savings: $215k
