“Every piece of feedback matters”: how Edison captures & processes user feedback

“Every piece of feedback matters”: how Edison captures & processes user feedback

by Tatiana Ugriumova, Edison Product Owner

Outlining how you can easily submit feedback and ideas to the Edison platform, and what happens to these items after you do

Published: Dec 8, 2020


Edison: versatile web publishing for all of Pfizer

The Edison platform is a busy place. We have almost 20k code commits on GitHub per month by developers working on sites built on Edison, and changes happening on different parts of the platform multiple times a day (you can see everything on our Changelog). Like all software teams, we want to make sure we’re building something that is high quality and meets our users’ needs.

With that in mind, one of the things we’re proud of is how we process user feedback. We know feedback underpins product improvement – after all, as product management guru Marty Cagan says, without it you’re just guessing - so we have an easy way for our users to submit their thoughts, and transparency & organization in what we do with these insights.

We created a special Jira board called Edison Ideas:

Edison Ideas board kanban view

Edison Ideas board backlog

What’s this board used for?

Well, first things first – if you have a bug or a support request, you should email DL-BT-DIGITALMARKETING@pfizer.com.

If you have an idea, suggestion, or improvement request, however, you use the Edison Ideas board. This means you can suggest new functionality or enhancements for the Edison platform to ensure you – as an Edison user - can continue to meet the needs of the business in your work.

Why do we do this? And why do we do it in this way?

We want to optimize for success on two counts:

  • Making sure our users’ opinions get heard in a single, transparent forum

  • Making sure we operationalize the feedback in a way that doesn’t get lost in (or create extra noise in) our existing Product Roadmap

Every piece of feedback matters, and we use it to inform our work after it’s been triaged through the Edison Ideas process. Not every submitted idea will be implemented, but all of them are valuable and all are welcome, as they let our team identify patterns and trends and modify our roadmap accordingly.

How can I get involved? How do I submit an idea?

You simply have to request access to our Edison Ideas board by contacting me (Tatiana Ugriumova) on Teams. Unfortunately, we’re not able to make the board public, but we add everyone who asks. Then you can open a ticket and submit your idea!

Write a clear summary (title), and please put as much helpful content, links, pictures etc. in the description to help with refining:

​​​​​​​Make sure you specify which offering or part of the platform your idea relates to, and why exactly you think it’s needed. This is particularly important if it relates to any projects or initiatives currently in progress or with looming deadlines, or if you think it’s really urgent.

Keeping you in the loop

As you’ll now be on this board, you’ll be marked as the “Reporter” - which means any updates to the ticket will trigger an email to you.

The process is:

  • You submit a ticket

  • I get a notification and personally review every single submission

  • If necessary, I ask for clarification or more information

  • After the ticket description is clear, a number of things may happen:

    • If there’s a better route to addressing your concern (for instance, another platform is better placed to help) I’ll point you in the right direction to hand over to them

    • If the idea is good, but technically not feasible or very difficult to implement well, I’ll explain why we can’t do it and potentially some workarounds

    • If the idea is something we’re already working on, I’ll let you know what you can expect and when

    • If the idea is valid and feasible, I’ll cross-reference with our Roadmap and see if we can implement your idea at the same time as something it relates to

    • If the idea is particularly urgent (for instance a COVID-related site at the moment) I’ll do my best to get it into the development team’s backlog asap, and tell you the expected timeline. (This is why a clear description is so essential!)

Lastly, if the idea is valid and feasible, but not urgent, and is not related to any of the current roadmap initiatives, it’ll stay in our Ideas Board. We aren’t able to implement every single idea that comes along, even if they’re really good ideas. However, we regularly review all the ideas waiting on this Board, especially before updating our roadmap. Sometimes something might increase in priority over time, or we might identify a trend with many ideas about a particular area, and then we can formulate a roadmap item around them.

It’s not possible to give an average turnaround time – as you can imagine, the feedback is very varied, so sometimes things are actioned in a matter of weeks, and sometimes they are on the board for a lot longer – but the important thing is they don’t simply disappear into a black box after submission, and they actively inform our roadmap and team direction.

If you have any suggestions or feedback, please find me on Teams, send me an email (Tatiana.Ugriumova@Pfizer.com) or submit a ticket to our Edison Ideas Board.
