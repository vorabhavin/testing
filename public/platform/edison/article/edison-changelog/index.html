How Edison's Changelog solution can help your team

How Edison's Changelog solution can help your team

by Tatiana Ugriumova, Edison Product Owner

Tatiana Ugriumova, Product Owner for Edison, explains how they solved the problem of keeping everyone up-to-date in a busy ecosystem

Published: Dec 17, 2021

Edison: versatile web publishing for all of Pfizer

The problems of coordinating comms in a busy ecosystem

The Edison platform is a busy place. We have over a thousand sites across 100+ markets, with dozens of development teams working on them around the clock. Many different teams are involved in delivering a site from client partners to solution center owners, vendor developers, operations team, security & analytics teams, and more.

At the same time, things are constantly changing.

The Edison development team releases 4-5 updates to various parts of the platform ecosystem per week; infrastructure changes, new policies or process changes are introduced; some tools or services reaching end-of-life stage. It is hard to keep up. In addition, each of these changes can potentially disrupt somebody else’s work.

A few years ago, we had a real problem with people constantly missing important announcements (or even simple platform releases). As a result, development work and MTPs were often disrupted because teams did not realize something happened the day before or was scheduled to happen tomorrow.

People were frustrated. This was the constant feedback we got from vendor teams and many others.

Finding the solution for information equity

To address these problems, we came up with the Edison Changelog: a central tool to communicate any and all changes in the Edison ecosystem. It provides a source of truth for all changes, and ensures important information is communicated clearly and ahead of time.

Change records

This was how the changelog started. We simply wanted a single place to show all the changes that the teams working with the Edison platform would need to know about. Unlike a changelog for a piece of software, our definition of “change” is much wider. Here we announce new platform releases, as well as infrastructure changes, new or updated policies, changes to important platform processes, as well as end of life for tools or services we use.

The changes are posted as discrete entries (‘change records’) for every type of change (End of life, Infrastructure, Policy change, Process, Release) with a synopsis of the changes, the date it takes effect and who to contact with questions or concerns.

Per our internal process, a change record is published at least 2 days ahead of the date of the change. For really impactful and disruptive changes we usually give even more lead time (up to 2-4 weeks).

To ensure our audience is notified of new records, we send an email notification every time a new change record is published. Anyone can subscribe to receive these notifications on the Changelog’s front page. We also automatically subscribe those with active accounts on the Edison Dashboard. Currently, we have 560 people receiving notifications of Edison changes.

Blog

After posting change records for a while, we wanted a way to share long form writing, and thus a Blog section was added. Here we share detailed announcements of the new features, how to guides, our sprint summaries, case studies, product collaborations, and other posts.

Outages

This is the newest addition to the Changelog. While change records were very successful in communicating planned changes, we realized that there was another type of temporary change that often disrupts our user’s work: outages. Having timely information about outages in the same place as the upcoming or past changes would give the teams the full view of the environment, and allow them to adapt to what is happening.

So similar to changes, we created a single place for all outages (planned or unplanned) related to Edison platform. This functionality is currently in beta testing and will be enabled on Edison Changelog shortly.

An outage record includes summary of the issue, which parts of the Edison ecosystem the outage affects, when it started and when it is expected to be resolved. Regular updates to the outage will be posted on the record itself, so that it is a source of truth and shows up-to-date information at any time. Email notifications will go out for both new outages and updates to existing outages.

The impact of having a central communications hub

  1. It’s improved transparency between the Edison platform and our users We launched the initial version of the Changelog – showing just change records - in March 2018, and it’s been a real success. Over the next few months, we stopped receiving so many questions about when something was going to happen, or why something had happened unannounced. Almost 2 years on, we are now no longer getting those questions at all.

  2. It’s provided valuable information for teams to use however they need Teams use the information in different ways. Most people may get the necessary updates from our email notifications, but there are also teams that review different change records together weekly during their team meetings.

  3. It’s provided our users and us with a historical timeline of important changes It is often very helpful to be able to go back and see when exactly certain releases came out or certain changes happened. It’s proven useful when development teams start working on the site that hasn’t been updated for a whole, for the Operations team debugging issues, and even for our own engineering team as well.

What’s next

The value of technology lies primarily in automation, innovation, and speed. To that note, we’re ambitious about expanding the Changelog solution into more of a real-time operational hub. The addition of Outages has been the first step in this transformation – which will make the changelog even more valuable as a tool to communicate between Edison and our users.

Going beyond Edison

After the Edison Changelog had been around for a while, we started getting questions about it from other teams - all platform teams face similar problems, after all. As a result, we set aside some time and re-architected our Changelog to make it re-usable.

The Changelog itself is an Edison Custom website. In order to make it easily re-usable for other platforms, we moved all the functionality into a standalone Drupal module: “Changelog Core”. This means we can easily spin up a new Edison Custom site, enable that module, and voila – you get all the functionality, as well as the future changes and enhancements that will be released.

Electron, Biblio, and Portal NextGen are currently already using it, and we are currently preparing their own changelogs for a couple more platform teams. There are a number of reasons we are excited about this: it prioritizes end user experience, it standardizes and adds predictability to user experience across Platform DevOps, it demonstrates that the platforms are adopting unifying processes, working more closely together. Lastly, it means platform teams don’t need to reinvent the wheel, which saves the whole company money.
