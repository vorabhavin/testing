Scaling a global COVID-19 study site in seven days: Part 3 - What We Learned

Scaling a global COVID-19 study site in seven days: Part 3 - What We Learned

by Platform DevOps

Published: Feb 16, 2021

 In the final instalment of the COVID-19 study site blog series, we look at what we have learned from this fast-paced site build and what we would do differently next time we are faced with a similar time-critical challenge.

The Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine study site - www.covidvaccinestudy.com - underwent a dramatic and fast-paced transformation by C&CCC in the week leading up to launch.

In Part 1 and Part 2 of this series we looked at the challenges we faced and how we dealt with them. Here, we continue to look at this unique project and explore what we have learned and the ideal architecture for future scenarios.

What we learned

Despite this being an incredible challenge, it was also a lesson - a “good example” - of a site that didn’t have quite the right architecture at the start. Fixing this was stressful, time-consuming, and resource-intensive - but there are lessons to be taken away from this experience, so it doesn’t have to happen again.

  1. This was unprecedented. But high profile, high traffic sites will be a repeat occurrence and we must use the right approach and architecture
  2. C&CCC has that architecture: our platforms are optimized to work together and solve unusual, demanding digital problems like this, as well as more straightforward business needs
  3. Every so often you have to step up, individually and as a team, and do what’s needed to make everyone successful. Globally distributed working means challenging time zones and working hours for people pulling extraordinary efforts - but working together closely and flexibly can mitigate this
  4. In addition, it’s an overwhelmingly positive testament to the collaboration, skills and knowledge of the assorted C&CCC team that pulled together to bring this project to where it needed to be - including those who didn’t work directly on it but kept everything ticking over smoothly in the background. With key people pulled off their usual demanding workloads to work 90 hour weeks on this COVID-19 vaccine study site, the wider C&CCC team also stepped up and ensured nothing fell through the gaps

We’d love this event to be one-of-a-kind: this kind of reactionary firefighting is commendable but isn’t sustainable. Part of our growth must be to move beyond events like these; making sure they don’t happen again, and not only being able to “pull it out of the bag” when it does happen. This isn’t just about delivery - this is about keeping teams on track, finding problems ahead of time, being able to maneuver and respond in an agile way and a manageable timeframe.

What can be done differently in the future?

  • Design to scale, even if you might not need it
  • Test frequently, and as part of the build process. Tests should go hand in hand with building, not retrofitted
  • Use the architectural patterns and processes proven by C&CCC - for instance, using a combination of the Edison platform’s Lite offering (simple, static webpage, no CMS infrastructure) interfacing with Stratus’s microservices would be the perfect solution for a problem like the COVID-19 vaccine study site (see an example here). Edison also offers encryption for database-stored data by default - another example of something that was missing before and is critical
    ​​​​​​​

Many thanks to the entire team who made this a reality, and to Mike Lamb, Eric Silva, Dave Hall, Chad DeGroot and Al Nutile for their insight on this experience.
