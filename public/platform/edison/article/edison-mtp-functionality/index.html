Announcing automated MTP functionality

Announcing automated MTP functionality

by Tatiana Ugriumova, Edison Product Owner

Tatiana Ugriumova, Edison Product Owner, explains how their user-friendly automated MTP process changes the game.

Published:
Nov 24, 2020
​​​​
​​​​​​​

Understanding the problem

In web publishing, websites - especially those in a highly-regulated and high-impact field like global pharma - must go through numerous tests to ensure accuracy, compliance, and security. Once the team is satisfied that the site content or feature is safe, predictable, and strongly meets the end user’s needs, they can “move to production” (MTP). This is usually the last step before going completely “live”.

This MTP process involves much paperwork, numerous people, and an incredible number of manual tasks; a typical MTP requires 45 distinct steps and sometimes a site might need more than one MTP to launch a new feature. On top of that, because it is such a time consuming process, vendor teams charge for preparing every MTP and thus business owners incur additional costs. It’s one of the most expensive, complicated, time-consuming processes involved in launching and maintaining a website.

Edison's MTP process is now automated


Finding the best solution

In 2019 alone, the Operations team performed 4250 MTPs across all three Edison Offerings (Lite, Custom and Legacy) at an annual cost of about $500,000. As well as this financial incentive, we were very aware that the MTP process is one of the most cumbersome experiences for users of our platform.

We looked at multiple analyses of information and it was clear that this problem needed an ambitious re-think. If we didn’t find the right way to fix this, Pfizer’s web publishing capabilities would fall behind in the industry; as the primary web publishing platform, improving this would have a direct impact on our winning the digital race in pharma.

Why we’re thrilled about it

It’s much easier and faster for development teams to get an MTP done now themselves any time they need to, as opposed to having to go through an Operations Team. It also has the added benefits for Pfizer of being more streamlined and more cost-efficient!

We’ve drastically improved the user experience and we’re saving a significant amount of money. Equally importantly, by improving this process we’re ensuring our users can release code to production more frequently, and potentially in smaller chunks. This MTP upgrade embodies the essence of agile working and brings our workflows closer to modern agile software goals: small, swift iterations with plenty of scope for bringing feedback on board.

Looking to the future

This functionality is now available for all Edison sites. As of the week starting November 23rd, 1,436 MTPs have been completed since we launched 3.5 months ago - an increase of 62% on the 885 completed the previous 3 months, and rising all the time as people are able to complete MTPs both more frequently and more easily.

In the future, we’re looking at automating some of the remaining manual steps around the SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) process. Thanks to the success of this MTP automation, we are looking at automating additional operational tasks in the near future.
