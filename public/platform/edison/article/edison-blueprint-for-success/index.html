A Blueprint for Success: our ideal architecture

A Blueprint for Success: our ideal architecture

by Platform DevOps

Bringing together learnings from across the COVID era, the Customer & Commercial Creation Center (C&CCC) team outlines the ideal technical architecture for secure, scalable digital assets.

Published: Jan 9, 2021

Why is this important?

The COVID pandemic has changed the dynamics of problems and the way we solve them. It’s always been important to provide secure websites, but now we’re thinking about how to deliver them at speed, and at scale.

Scalability

This word comes up a lot, so let’s make sure we’re on the same page. Scalability is the property of something to handle a growing (or shrinking) workload, by adding or removing resources with ease. You don’t simply install inflexible, large capacity architecture and wash your hands of it; that’s grossly inefficient. The smart approach is to solve the problem of fluctuations in service need with flexibility, allowing you to scale up and down quickly and easily depending on your requirements.

Scalability is intrinsically linked to performance.

Performance at scale: providing smart solutions

Something both imperative and impressive, for any software service, is being able to scale and keep the performance level consistently high no matter the load or traffic.

Recently, Pfizer was faced with a problem just like this. We know how to make excellent public-facing websites for clinical trial volunteers. But building a site for a COVID vaccine study adds two major extra dimensions: how do we build the same thing, but capable of handling up to (again, worst case predicted scenario) two million visitors to the site at the same time, and keep their submitted form data consistently safe and secure?

The ideal set-up

As with anything in life, having the right approach and the appropriate infrastructure goes a very long way. The Customer & Commercial Creation Center (C&CCC) team benefits from the combined expertise of a lot of smart people - and we’re confident we have just the right architecture for this type of problem. This is important for Pfizer because high profile, high traffic sites will recur, and confidence in a foolproof template is a Must Have for winning the digital race in pharma.

The C&CCC blueprint for success for these kinds of assets is: a website built on Edison Lite which integrates with Stratus microservices.

Why?

1. It’s flexible

  • Edison Custom, though very powerful and great for content management, has a couple of drawbacks in this situation. It runs, like many web solutions, on a ‘stack’ (like a suite or bundle - a complete set of software to address a problem) with the acronym LAMP: Linux operating system, Apache web server, MySQL database, and a PHP-language scripting layer. As code is always evolving, it’s a non-negotiable truth that application servers have to be maintained and kept up to date to maintain expected, consistent performance. The database itself can also be a problem: either proving to be a bottleneck as traffic increases, or sometimes a point of actual failure

  • In comparison, Edison Lite runs a serverless stack: one where all the infrastructure management tasks are handled by a cloud provider. They allocate resources as needed - like going to the supermarket and deciding at the door whether you need a basket or a trolley depending on how long your shopping list is. One of the best things about this approach is that it’s infinitely scalable - and this flexibility in dependable resourcing is essential for supporting sites where the number of users may fluctuate from two right up to two million

  • The microservices aspect dovetails perfectly with this. Microservices describes an architectural approach to building software. It focuses on building single-function, highly-performant modules which integrate seamlessly to build something bigger - like the individual Lego pieces which make up the toy Apollo Saturn V rocket. It’s the opposite of a “monolithic” approach such as Edison Custom, where there is one database performing all roles, from serving content through processing logins and capturing form submissions. In a monolith, if any of these are overwhelmed, the whole service goes down

  • With a microservices approach, we can completely isolate these components, so those that are easier to scale (such as serving content) can keep running flawlessly while others (such as receiving and processing form submissions) might need more attention when scaling. This gives us much more flexibility to quickly respond and make individual changes to allow a specific component to scale

2. It’s fast and reliable

  • Sites like these usually host primarily static content (information, text) with a handful of functional features, like signing up for something. Because Edison have streamlined and really honed the delivery of these sites, delivery time is incredibly quick. You can build and deploy an Edison Lite site in half the time of an Edison Custom site because it’s perfect for the job without needing extra customization, complexity or idiosyncrasy

  • The uptime for Edison Lite sites is incredibly impressive: >99.99% across all Lite sites since we started building sites using it

  • It’s reliable and safe, as Edison sites are built with Cloudflare protection as standard. A CDN (Content Delivery Network) is a collection of globally distributed servers which aims to optimize performance of content delivery even though the consumers may be spread over a large geographical area. We put Cloudflare (who provide industry-leading web infrastructure and security) in front of our AWS infrastructure to provide increased security - immediately, we gained DDoS protection (a Distributed Denial of Service attack is one of the most common forms of cyber attack), a web application firewall, and more

3. It’s progressive, and represents a game-changing improvement

  • In some senses, using a combination of Edison Lite and microservices is a paradigm shift in how we build solutions to complicated problems

  • As a solution, it arguably adds complexity in that we have to reimagine the way we think about and build web applications. But code is always evolving, and there were already complexities and challenges in the old solutions. Constantly having to read/write to the database, keeping all copies of the database and all code up to date, constantly patching code - we’ve entered an era where that doesn’t need to be the reality for all digital solutions

  • The concept of serverless isn’t new, but has only started moving towards mass adoption recently, as peoples’ needs have changed. We’re building the solutions that will constitute the new generation of digital solutions

The added benefits of being a combination of two high-functioning proprietary solutions is, of course:

  • Integration between the two = customizable and flexible
  • Built-in compliance solutions
  • Part of the Pfizer Digital ecosystem, integration with other tools marketeers use
  • In-house support with the most knowledgeable Subject Matter Experts - the people who built the platforms themselves and regularly improve it

Taking the long view

This isn’t just an approach for right now, in the COVID era. In the majority of cases, successful systems, sites and projects expect to see an increase in traffic - it’s a standard Key Performance Indicator commonly used as a synonym for growth.

The C&CCC team is committed to providing highly performant, easy to adopt, and - where necessary - innovative solutions for an incredible variety of business problems. We do all the heavy lifting and confirming the right foundations, so you don’t have to.
