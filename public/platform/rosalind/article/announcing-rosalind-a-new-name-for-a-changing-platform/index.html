Announcing Rosalind: A new name for a changing platform. 

Announcing Rosalind: A new name for a changing platform.

By Hugh Figden

Published: 19th April 2021

Rosalind is the new name for C&CCC’s platform for customer data management, campaign definition and omnichannel activation.  

Rosalind is the new name for C&CCC’s platform for customer data management, campaign definition and omnichannel activation.  

You may recognize some of these capabilities in MAP, PMAP, SSE and Marketo, but C&CCC’s capabilities are changing and expanding, and we needed a recognizable name for this area of platform capability that allows us to grow, without being tied to any single tool, product, or service. 

We needed a name that brings our team together and makes it easy for other teams to connect with us, makes it easier to get help, request enhancements, and find documentation.  

What is Rosalind?

Rosalind’s platform vision is to define and manage C&CCC’s capabilities for customer data management, campaign definition and omnichannel activation. This encompasses three areas: 

UNIFY - Rosalind Customer Management 

Customer Acquisition & Management (GRV, PMAP, Newton Connect, MDM Integration) 
Channel Consent (GCP, PMAP, SSE, Newton Connect) 
Data Exchange Hubs (DEH, Eventhub) 

DEFINE - Rosalind Campaign Definition 

Campaign Metadata (CMT) 
Segmentation/Personalization (TLT, Marketo, Thunderhead, GSR) 
Journey Orchestration (Thunderhead, Marketo) ​​​​​​​

DEPLOY - Rosalind Automation  

MAP/PMAP (Marketo Email) 
Multi-Channel Automation (Vendor Hub Direct Mail, Web, SMS) 
Key Integrations (MDM, Adobe, Analytics EcoSystem, Canvas, Veeva) 
Journey Orchestration (Thunderhead + Canvas, Edison & Newton Connect integrations) ​​​​​​​

 Many elements of this vision already exist, some managed by Rosalind, some managed by partners who we will continue to work with as part of our integrated platform ecosystem.  

Other elements of the above are yet to be built, e.g. our Journey Orchestration capability, which will be developed 2021-2022 in partnership with the Customer Experience organisation and other C&CCC teams. 

Why Rosalind?

Our other platforms have strong identifiers and brands. Edison. Stratus. Newton. Force.com.  

We needed a similarly strong identifier for the marketing automation space as we found ourselves saying things like “PMAP, maintained by the MAP team” and “MAP, maintained by the Marketing Automation team”.  

With Pfizer’s new vision, Bold Moves and the need for considerable renewed investment in capabilities in this area, we needed a name. 

We chose to name this platform after Rosalind Franklin. 

Female scientists have made hugely important contributions to science; however, their names have historically not been as visible and honored as those of male scientists. By recognising the contributions of Rosalind Franklin we honour her work, and step towards a more diverse mix of product names in C&CCC. 

Rosalind Franklin’s experiments and discoveries with X-ray crystallography enabled scientists in fields related to Pfizer’s purpose. Her work on imaging the structure of DNA was critical in determining that it was a double-helix, a discovery that earned James Watson and Francies Crick a Nobel prize but for which Rosalind received no official acknowledgement.

X-ray crystallography clearly pictured the structure of things previously not able to be seen. DNA is the famous example, but it was also key in describing the structure of Penicillin, a drug that Pfizer famously mass produced in the 1940s.

Today, we are putting Rosalind’s name at the heart of our efforts, reflecting and celebrating the crucial work she carried out. The Rosalind Platform products (MAP/PMAP and Newton) will enable breakthrough digital products to be created. The Platform allows marketers to get a clearer picture of customer, campaign, and interaction data. This is becoming ever more critical and relevant to our business aims as a company.
