An Overview of Biblio: in conversation with Dick Olsson

An Overview of Biblio: in conversation with Dick Olsson

by Dick Olsson, Biblio Product Owner

Published: Jan 10, 2021

Biblio is an application that provides an automated inventory of all Platform DevOps & Global Digital Operations assets: apps, websites, and functional components. It is a functional layer on top of CMDB which provides rich, detailed digital metadata used to maintain, secure and retire assets as needed. It saves Pfizer time and money, and operates compliantly as it is built in-house.

Dick, what’s your role at Pfizer?

I do many different things at Pfizer. My primary role is being the Support Lead for all Digital Patient Solutions, such as patient-facing websites and mobile apps. However, one of my secondary roles is being the Product Owner of Biblio.

What is Biblio?

Biblio is an application built on top of the Electron platform in Pfizer Digital. It provides a way for Global Digital Operations (GDO) and Platform DevOps to keep an automated inventory bibliotheque (library) of the GDO code-based assets: apps, websites, and functional components. This means they can better support, maintain, secure, and retire assets as needed – they can operate more seamlessly, and take decisions with better data at hand.

Why is Biblio important to Pfizer?

Pfizer builds and hosts an incredible number of digital assets. We support 1000+ websites and 200+ mobile apps – and when you include development and staging environments, those numbers multiply by 3 or 4 times. On top of that, the Stratus Platform – which builds custom digital applications - are also building microservices to add functionality to our web solutions.

The tool that Pfizer currently has in place - CMDB (Configuration Management Database) - is the “system of record” for all assets at Pfizer. It is very deeply embedded in Pfizer and the Business Technology department and supports a large number of legacy systems that must be maintained.

My team and the CMDB team work hand-in-hand and are closely aligned, because Biblio is a layer built on top of CMDB. Everything in the Platform DevOps (PDO) portfolio will exist as an entry in CMDB. As an older system, CMDB requires manual input and wasn’t built for the way IT systems now run – these days, IT is a lot more fast-paced and less predictable. In an agile environment, we need to be able to have automation helping us where we can. If we need to rapidly deploy 26+ COVID-related sites (which we did very recently) we need to have all relevant information automatically captured, saving the business money, and then provided in one place for it to be smoothly manually entered into CMDB.

Biblio helps us keep CMDB up to date, more accurately, in real time, even when faced with exponential growth of entries in an increasingly digital world. They aren’t in competition and have clear product remits; if anything, Biblio is an assistive product which makes CMDB better.

How does Biblio fit into the bigger picture of Pfizer Digital?

Biblio helps support a healthy, efficient, consistent digital ecosystem. We provide inventory owners with accurate data on the status of code-based assets. In turn, they can support developers, websites, and apps appropriately, and make better-informed decisions.

As an inventory, Biblio is closely integrated with CMDB but is positioned to have a better view on our PDO platforms and portfolio. It collects a lot of additional metadata not stored in CMDB that means that the Operations team has better quality data to make decisions. For instance, security and patching are essential. If you have 400 websites in front of you, how do you know which are the ones that are the most important, or the most in need? You need that information to prioritize and work smarter, with higher impact and efficacy.

Because Biblio is built on top of the Electron platform, it means we automatically benefit from Electron’s event-driven architecture. This programming paradigm provides a robust, reactive structure for automation – put simply, it’s a system which responds to any changes in “state”. The advantage of event-driven systems is that records are kept historically (as a log) instead of being over-written, so you can always go back and see the history, and the system responds immediately when something changes, rather than waiting for something to tell it to fetch or search for changes. For example, the kinds of state or status changes it tracks are: assignments of domain names to all assets, or the status of an asset (e.g. new, build, run, decommissioned, retired).

What are the benefits of Biblio?

The value Biblio adds for solutions is clear:

  • Better and more responsive support for applications and projects

  • Better security (we can only patch the apps we know we have!)

  • Better compliance as we can centralize processes & updates

There are a couple of technical things I really want to draw attention to here:

It’s built to be agile: Biblio is a library of many small registers that together make up what’s considered the complete asset inventory. Being composed of many small and loosely coupled registers is in itself one of the main features – look out for future blogposts explaining why.

It’s comprehensive and accessible: Another important feature of Biblio is the rich, well-documented, secure API through which all platforms and teams can query its inventory data. This further enhances security, efficiency, and automation potential. 

Who would use Biblio? Who is it aimed at?

Primarily, colleagues in GDO who manage the inventory of our assets. These inventory managers keep track of all our websites and mobile apps: who built them, where they are hosted, who owns them, who fixes them, etc.

What is the Biblio team currently working on?

The Biblio roadmap should be complete within the next year or two. The main element is to automate all essential aspects of the GDO inventory across a few key platforms mentioned before; Edison, Newton, CMDB and Cloudflare. 

At the moment we do not include .NET sites or Sharepoint sites. We may include them in the future. 

Give an example of a project or process using Biblio.

We are currently automating the retirement of websites. Ensuring retired websites are removed is arguably as important, in terms of potential end user benefits and impact, as getting new ones out. This piece of automation needs to automatically fetch the details for business owner for each site; Biblio provides an API that enables this. 

What makes you work successfully as a team?

As a team, we are committed to delivering to a high standard, supporting each other and continuously improving. We do this by focusing on process excellence when it comes to our development practices. For example, we work according to lean principles and closely track the Four Key DevOps Metrics with all the work we do.

How can people reach you and learn more about Biblio?
