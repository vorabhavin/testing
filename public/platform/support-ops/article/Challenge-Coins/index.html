Challenge Coins: Celebrating and Remembering our Digital Success Stories 

Challenge Coins: Celebrating and Remembering our Digital Success Stories 

by Andy Garcia, Global Operations Lead

Published: May 12th, 2021

Platform DevOps recognizes successful project teams with the production of challenge coins 


​​​​​​​Platform DevOps has released its first challenge coins to recognize the team members of successful digital projects in 2020. Colleagues involved in Digital Fortress, Project Neo, Edison Lite Migration and Virtual Core will be the first to receive the celebratory coins.  

Why are we doing this? Because these projects wouldn't be a success without the Platform DevOps teams' expertise and dedication. And we want to highlight and recognize their efforts.  
​​​​

    Meet the Projects: 

    HCP Virtual Core Portal (2.x): One of the first Bold Moves Experiments to enable the Field Force and pioneer a new customer interaction method.   
    Digital Fortress - 2020: Responsible for delivering results across 14 separate workstreams that enhanced site security, managed deprecated components, upgraded platform versions, completed asset retirements, and ensured inventory integrity.   
    Edison Lite Migration: Successfully migrated all Legacy SiteFactory and non-complex Legacy D7 sites from the Legacy platforms to a lower cost, more efficient solution.   
    Project Neo: Provided Day 1 Business Continuity for websites, mobile apps and digital marketing tools in the event of the Upjohn business unit separation.  

    About the Coins

    Each coin depicts the year, project name and digital platform involved. The more successful projects you are involved with, the more coins you can collect!  

    Why a coin?

    Coins have been used throughout history to recognize achievements or act as morale boosters. It's believed Romans started the custom by providing coins to their military. After each day in battle, soldiers in Ancient Rome earned an additional coin if they fought with exceptional skill and determination.  

    Where does the "challenge" idea originate? 

    "The challenge" is said to have started after the Second World War in Germany. Americans stationed there began to conduct "pfennig checks," (the pfennig was the lowest-denomination German coin). If you could not produce a pfennig when someone called a check, you had to buy the beers! 
     
    The pfennig checks later evolved to a unit medallion check, with unit members challenging one another by slamming their medallions down on a bar. Any member who lacked their medallion had to purchase drinks for everyone else who had their coins. Meanwhile, if everyone could present a medallion, the challenger was the one who had to buy the drinks.  


    While we're not expecting owners of Platform DevOps Challenge Coins to gain more in battle or even get the drinks in, we hope the Coins bring joy and pride to all those that receive them.
