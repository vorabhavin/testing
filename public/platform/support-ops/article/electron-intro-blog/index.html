An overview of Electron: in conversation with Dick Olsson

An overview of Electron: in conversation with Dick Olsson

by Dick Olsson, Electron Product Owner

Published: Jan 5, 2021

Executive summary:
Electron is a digital platform that excels in turning repetitive tasks into shared tooling. Pfizer Digital and, in turn, all of Pfizer, benefits through this innovative automation of multiple processes. It not only frees up valuable talent to continue to do what they do best, but also means we can move faster towards making digital solutions which improve patients’ lives. In the past year alone, we’ve achieved:
Consistent 99.99% uptime across our automated process supporting Platform DevOps
Saved Pfizer more than $514,000 in avoided costs
100% of all security fixes deployed within 24h of discovery

Electron: smart automation for Pfizer Digital​​​​​​​


​​​​​​​

Dick, what’s your role at Pfizer?

I do many different things at Pfizer. My primary role is being the Support Lead for all Digital Patient Solutions, such as patient-facing websites and mobile apps. However, one of my secondary roles is being the Product Owner of the Electron platform.

What is Electron?

Electron is a platform within the Platform DevOps team. In one sentence, it turns repetitive tasks into shared tooling. It standardizes, automates, and optimizes workflows across all the teams that provide digital platforms for Pfizer’s internal developers.

Our aim is to ensure everyone in the organization has the maximum impact on delivering the best digital services and experiences improving patient welfare. We do this by constantly looking for and acting upon opportunities for automation, optimization, and standardization across digital workflows. We explicitly avoid expanding our offering: our goal is to do a few things very well.

Why is Electron important to Pfizer?

In order to enable Pfizer to create breakthroughs, we believe it’s paramount to let stakeholders and platform developers focus their time, resources and attention on what’s important and meaningful.

Having the ability to automate tasks and avoid costs of manual tasks is what Electron enables. It is through productivity and efficiency gains that Electron will help Pfizer to win the digital race in pharma. 

How does Electron fit into the bigger picture of Pfizer Digital?

Every day, the world works on more and better digital solutions, and Pfizer digital is no different. We want to continue to provide best-in-class options. With increasing complexity and increasing integrations between platforms, this way of working with automation is the only way forward. It’s a commitment to agile workflows and loosely-coupled systems, which we really feel is the way forward. We provide a way for integrations and processes to have the heavy lifting done by automation.

What problems or challenges does Electron solve?

  1. It standardizes the language that our applications use to speak to each other
    Electron’s “event system” is a really important feature, and we’ll be writing about this more in the future. The event system lets developers build small and simple services that enable different applications to communicate with each other through standardized messages (a.k.a. events) in a way that’s loosely coupled (where the parts don’t need to have intimate working knowledge of each other), making it thus more robust and easier to maintain.

  2. It reduces manual repetition in digital workflows
    Another important aspect is Electron’s “task system” which enables developers, with just a few lines of Python code, to create long-running tasks. Electron takes care of the orchestration, deployment and execution of tasks in a way that makes teams more productive, thus saving time and resources. 

  3. It offers a multitude of ways to improve developer achievement
    Lastly, the Electron platform provides a suite of productivity items for developers such as local development tools, standardized logging, error handling and debugging capabilities. The benefit is that it’s easier to build more robust systems that are easier to maintain. But there is also less duplication of effort, making individual developers more productive, thus also saving time and resources. 

What are the benefits of Electron?

The benefits extend to a range of different internal users. Electron supports platform developers in delivering true impact, lifting the burden of repetitive tasks from their shoulders. It pushes for best practice and quality in Platform DevOps, especially through the use of the event and task management systems which encourage loosely coupled, services-based architecture. Together, this saves Pfizer costs and staff time by making applications more robust and maintainable, and preventing duplication.

Who would use Electron? Who is Electron aimed at?

Primary target users of the Electron platform are other Platform DevOps or Customer & Commercial Creation Center developers. The goal is to drive cost avoidance by building and operating a platform on which it’s easy and cheap to automate processes. 

What is the Electron team currently working on?

In the short term, Electron will continue to enable and deliver on automation projects such as the Automated Move To Production (MTP) process for Edison and Automated Website Decommissioning via Biblio.

In the long term, Electron will ensure that the platform is net positive in terms of enabled cost avoidance. This is done by not growing the offering too much and instead by providing a small and focused set of features, requiring only a small development team to build and maintain it. 

Actions to ensure this include closely monitoring the cost avoidance performance enabled by the platform, as well as ensuring the highest possible performance of the development team itself to keep running resource costs lean. 

Give some examples of what’s been successfully built on Electron.

A typical project is identified by an opportunity to make some process more productive, automated, faster or less costly. 

For example, the Global Digital Operations (GDO) team used to spend lots of manual labor responding to tickets for taking and delivering database backups to other C&CCC developers. The Electron Platform enabled the Edison team to automate the processes of taking and delivering database backups, thus saving GDO (and Pfizer as a whole) a lot of time and avoiding costs. 

Another example is the Biblio application. It relies on Electron in order to seamlessly automate and connect together data from different systems, creating an inventory of all Pfizer code-based assets.

What makes you work successfully as a team?

We keep developer experience front and center. The Electron team exist for the purpose of enabling the work of other stakeholders and teams. That’s why features and services provided by the Electron platform are centered around developer productivity and experience. 

As a team, we are committed to delivering to a high standard, supporting each other and continuously improving. We do this by focusing on process excellence when it comes to our development practices. For example, we work according to lean principles and closely track the Four Key DevOps Metrics with all the work we do.
