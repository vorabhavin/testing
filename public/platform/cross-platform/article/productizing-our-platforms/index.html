Productizing our Platforms

by Richard Jones

Introducing the new Pfizer Digital Experience Platform Wheel, and how Pfizer Digital continues to grow to meet customers' needs.

Published: Apr 28, 2021

Productizing our Platforms

In 2021, we have five platforms we build and maintain in Platform DevOps plus a comprehensive support and operations team. Each of our platforms has a well-defined purpose enabling Pfizer to build different types of digital services.

Over the last year, we’ve made significant progress in improving awareness of our platforms and capabilities by focusing on high-level communication to the business - in particular leadership, client partners, and others we refer to as "the commissioning audience". These are the key decision-makers that are looking for digital services to meet the needs of our customers and patients.
​​​​​​​
Whilst this audience does not directly decide on architecture or implementation details of digital services, it’s important they have an awareness of what we are capable of in-house with our platform strategy.

Capabilities at the Core

The standard offering of the most prominent vendors in the commercial world is their Digital Experience Platforms (DXP). Rather than talking about individual platforms like a CMS, the conversation is about how all of the different capabilities work seamlessly together to meet customer needs. In reality, these vendors have actively acquired other companies to complete their DXP map - leading to a patchwork of solutions with varying depth of integration.

When all of the platforms in Platform DevOps are combined with other parts of Pfizer Digital, we have the capabilities of a DXP - and uniquely have integration points key to Pfizer internal systems and processes as well. In my view, that puts us in the top right quadrant.

Moving Forward

The next phase of the journey is to focus on communicating the key benefits of our DXP to our audience - talking more about the capabilities that are relevant to the business and customer.
​​​​​​​
Our platforms are technically complex and have a wide array of capabilities. In order to ensure we can effectively communicate these, we can present common themes that are meaningful to our audience.

Faster, Better, Easier

Our platforms get us to market faster with a better quality product with key integrations ready to go making it easier to develop.

This is a simple message, but it can still be overwhelming when our platforms have so many capabilities. To address this, we now align these three core themes to each of our platforms, and within each theme, we have three products- bringing infinite possibilities to a manageable focus list.

This way, when talking about any of the platforms, we can easily map the benefits to capabilities, and highlight the values as a result.
​​​​​​​
The result is the Pfizer DXP wheel:

In an upcoming series of posts, we will detail how this model applies to each of the platforms.

Aligning Roadmaps

The added benefit of this approach is that we can align each platform roadmap to the DXP wheel. It should be possible to map any area of development to a theme and product in the wheel.
​​​​​​​
This, in turn, helps to visualize where our focus is across the whole team - and if something isn’t a fit to these values, gives us pause to consider whether it has a place on the roadmap.

Further Integration

Whilst DXP is ultimately a marketing term, our technical strategy continues to move towards cross-platform collaboration and integration - whether it be Edison Lite + Canvas, Force.com + Stratus, or other hybrids.

We will continue on our mission to keep demonstrating the value that Platforms bring to Pfizer and our part in winning the digital race in Pharma.
