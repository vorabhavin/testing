Introducing Stratus 

Introducing Stratus

by Platform DevOps

Published: Dec 1, 2020

An overview of Stratus: In conversation with Al Nutile Al Nutile - creator, Lead Engineer, and Product Owner on the Stratus platform - explains the platform itself, the solutions it provides, the theoretical framework that makes the team so successful.

Stratus overview

“Stratus is the foundation that allows customers to focus on building breakthrough apps, and not on infrastructure.”

Al, what’s your role at Pfizer? What does this involve?

I am the creator, Lead Engineer, and Product Owner on Stratus. My main role and goal is to try and understand what Stratus can do to continue to assist Pfizer in its digital transformation.

What is Stratus?

To remain competitive and excel in the market, organizations must accelerate. At the heart of this acceleration is software. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 36)

Stratus is one of the digital platforms in Platform DevOps run by Mike Lamb. Stratus is both a technology platform and a system of best practices that have been proven to build high performing technology applications. With Stratus, new teams and projects not only get started in a matter of hours but are also mentored in these strategies to enable Pfizer to create the right solutions faster without compromise.

Stratus is the foundation that allows customers to focus on building breakthrough apps, and not on infrastructure.

As a team, we focus especially on measuring our performance using the methods espoused in the well-known book mentioned above - “Accelerate” (the full title is “Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps: Building and Scaling High Performing Technology Organizations” and it’s known colloquially as the Accelerate movement). The book forms the theoretical backbone of our team, and could be used to do the same for other software teams. I like to tie everything we do, and how we go about doing it, back to this approach.

Why is Stratus important to Pfizer?

The third of W. Edwards Deming’s fourteen points for management states, “Cease dependence on inspection to achieve quality. Eliminate the need for inspection on a mass basis by building quality into the product in the first place” (Deming 2000). - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 82)

Stratus can get any project going with the right foundation. This foundation saves a project weeks of work, allowing the team to focus solely on the business goals of the project from day one. Since Stratus is a layer above all the infrastructure, we can provide more out of the box when compared with other cloud providers.

Along with getting projects going quickly, Stratus is designed to consider the long-term security and maintenance of projects as well. It uses Snyk and other automation tools to keep the software up to date and secure. The levels of automation we want to achieve on Stratus in security and quality are key to Pfizer’s digital transformation.

Stratus acceleration pathway

How does Stratus fit into the bigger picture of Pfizer Digital?

Following the theory developed by the Lean and Agile movements, implementing the practices of these movements can have an effect on culture. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 79)

Stratus fits into the bigger picture by proving the building blocks for any project type. These blocks are both the ready-to-go technology solution for things as common as authentication and scaling, and also the necessary training in the practices that are proven to build a culture of true agility. The Stratus development team operates at (and make it possible for others to operate at) the level of Elite Software Delivery Performers (see State of DevOps 2019 report, p. 18, which uses the Four Key Metrics of DevOps and we’ve written about elsewhere on the Stratus blog):

  • Deployment frequency: on demand (multiple deploys per day)

  • Lead time for changes: less than a day

  • Time to restore service: less than one hour

  • Change failure rate (how often a deployment doesn’t go as expected): 1-15%

Who would use Stratus? Who is it aimed at?

Effective management practices combined with technical approaches, such as continuous delivery, don’t just impact performance, they also have a measurable effect on organizational culture. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 156)

Pfizer developers who want to create bespoke apps quickly and easily use Stratus. A great example is the Rate My Meeting application. This was a project to allow employees, using their mobile devices, to rate the quality of a meeting. This project represents a common paradigm: the multifaceted solution needed to meet many business requirements at once. Firstly, this project needed a microservice application to provide the scalability - being able to serve the onslaught of users submitting votes, often simultaneously, at any time of the day. Second, it needed a web application to display management reports, as well as authentication and APIs for mobile devices.

All of this came together and fits perfectly into the workflows Stratus builds on. The Rate My Meeting team was able to deploy often to production, and test often, giving them the confidence that our systems could scale and pivot whenever necessary - as any project tends to do.

Furthermore, following the Rate My Meeting project Stratus has taken many of the newer patterns and solutions (like Cognito, AWS SAM, and more) and has integrated them into our templates so future projects would continue to benefit. And that is one of the biggest wins for Pfizer: having a platform that learns from its past successes and failures and makes sure to integrate the learnings into future projects.

What problems or challenges does Stratus solve?

Software and technology are key differentiators for organizations to deliver value to customers and stakeholders. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 36)

Stratus focuses on making the best building blocks so every new project can quickly build with those blocks and have confidence in the results. Security, Continuous Integration, Continuous Delivery, long term support, hands-off infrastructure - all of these are bundled together and ready to use on the platform straightaway. On top of this, onboarding of projects and teams is simplified as they now have one place to do all of their work no matter what cloud provider they are interacting with.

Projects that need a unique solution can use the building blocks we provide to fulfil a large portion of their goal. Stratus can then help them build and achieve the rest in a way that maintains the high level of software performance they need to be a successful product.

What are the benefits of Stratus?

Continuous delivery is a set of capabilities that enable us to get changes of all kinds—features, configuration changes, bug fixes, experiments—into production or into the hands of users safely, quickly, and sustainably. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 82)

I like to compare Stratus to Lego. The biggest benefit to every new project is that we can help developers build 80-90% of their short- and long-term goals. Authentication, documentation, onboarding, design patterns, infrastructure: these reusable blocks lead to more confidence from day one right up to the point where they can deployed to production, to truly meet the goals defined in the Accelerate Software Delivery Guidelines.

What is the Stratus team currently working on?

Stratus team has quite a few things on its plate right now. One is to make Stratus a hub of team building and community building. Stratus users will see others’ milestones in terms of skills and practices that are proven to make a healthy and high-performing software delivery team.

We are also making it more user friendly to quickly request a new project from Stratus. The new guided request process will allow the user to request a new application in just a few easy clicks.

And, of course, we are always working on automation improvements to ensure that active and non-active projects are kept secure and patched.

What makes you work successfully as a team?

Our analysis showed that the ability of teams to try out new ideas and create and update specifications during the development process, without requiring the approval of people outside the team, is an important factor in predicting organizational performance as measured in terms of profitability, productivity, and market share. - Forsgren PhD, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. Accelerate (p. 133)

We are constantly learning and striving to put in place well-known practices from the Accelerate movement. In that context, we are a team that strives to build good habits and not fear mistakes but try to learn from them.
