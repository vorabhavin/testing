Powering constant improvements: The benefits of building apps on Newton

Powering constant improvements: The benefits of building apps on Newton

by Newton Platform

Published: Feb 23, 2021

When a widely used build automation tool caused cascading problems at the end of 2020, we used our learnings in automation and platform-wide migration for a smooth transition to a better third-party alternative..

Running a responsive, responsible mobile development platform

Reflecting on challenging events brings learning and progress. The Newton team would like to shine a light on an achievement that demonstrates the benefits of our platform approach.

In late 2020, there were industry-wide problems with Travis-CI, a Continuous Integration (CI) service used to automatically build and test software projects triggered by source code changes. Pfizer used Travis to ensure that our applications and digital solutions were built and tested following software development best practice. This type of service forms a critical part of our Newton platform infrastructure by automating many of our processes and ensuring that only high-quality app code is deployed to our users.

In 2020, Travis made some impactful service changes that resulted in recurring service disruptions and outages and significant pricing increases.The changes disproportionately affected the Newton platform because we depended heavily on a now more expensive Apple build environment. These problems caused cascading impact to our platform clients in the form of delayed or failed builds and deployments, ultimately negatively affecting our end users. Newton was adding risk to other Customer & Commercial Creation Center (C4) platforms also attempting to work around new service conditions.

In response, the Newton Team set out to migrate to Microsoft’s GitHub Actions service. These efforts resulted in a migration to the new service via a rapidly automated process in just days. We also gained confirmation of an architecture design that prioritized simplicity and portability, in addition to many other valuable learnings. Newton Platform build processes are now all up and running on GitHub Actions for our four key mobile architectural patterns:

  • Native iOS
  • Native Android
  • Legacy, and
  • Current Hybrid frameworks
  • As legacy projects migrate to the Newton platform, they will automatically obtain this new service’s benefits
    ​​​​​​​

Benefiting from our expertise

Automation is central to the future of digital in Pfizer. Last year, the Newton team launched centralized continuous integration and automation as major improvements to Pfizer’s mobile platform. All key mobile app projects were migrated and have been benefiting since that time.

Once built, the team focused on a modular architecture comprised of small command-line utilities. The individual utilities are strung together in a scripted build process that performs these essential tasks on every mobile app’s build. From the organization of them to the simple scripting language in which they were written, the underlying structure of these tools contributed to a quick and painless migration. This reaffirmed our commitment to this architecture, and we were confident to continue with this pattern.

In that case, our development team can step in and perform that migration or new integration, for all projects, with minimal impact on the app delivery team.

The benefits of platform development

One of the powerful benefits offered by a platform is having experts on hand to keep an eye on what’s going on under the hood, enabling you to focus on things important to your users. The Newton Team works to actively and continually improve the platform and our processes to ensure that you and your team avoid pitfalls before you even know they exist.
