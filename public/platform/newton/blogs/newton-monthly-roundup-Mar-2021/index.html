'Overcoming unique challenges': Newton monthly roundup 

Newton/Marketing Automation monthly roundup

by Hugh Fidgen, Newton & Rosalind Platform Lead

Published: Apr 4, 2021


One thing I’ve learned on Newton is that the Mobile world is full of unique challenges. For example, unlike Edison or Stratus, Newton has two heavyweight external dependencies, Apple and Google, that regularly impose mandatory changes on us. And if we don’t react as they demand, they can break or even remove our production apps. Another example is that once an app has been downloaded by a user, our ability to change the code they’re running is limited. We effectively have to ask them to update our code. Forcing urgent changes through to users can be a real challenge therefore.

We’ve been living some of this pain for the last month, since Google changed a policy that affects how we’re able to use Google Social Login in our apps. Whilst the change actually makes sense from a privacy and security point of view, unfortunately it has not been straight forward for us to accommodate it and we’ve spent significant effort designing a response. There’s also no quick check for if our response is acceptable to Google other than “fixing” an app and trying to get it published through the review process to the Play Store. We wait for Google to respond and if it’s a rejection we decode why and plan what to do next. We’re nearly there, but such is the life of a Mobile DevOps team.

It’s a testament to the team, though, that in the midst of all this production churn, some excellent progress has been made behind the scenes.

Newton Pipeline is now firmly in an MVP state, able to push code all the way from code commit to app stores release. It bootstraps new projects with GitHub repos, AppCheck, and the rest of our platform skeleton to give developers a significant head start on their projects.

Newton Dashboard is also coming along at pace, in collaboration with the Stratus Dashboard team. We’ll be launching Beta access for our ops team this month and General Availability to C4 app development teams will follow in Q2. Early features will be asset management and metadata. Features that support developers in their daily tasks will quickly follow.

There’s plenty more going on too, of course. Auth0 migration; Component updates; Angular Schematics; Newton Connect improvements; Consultancy with Digital Companion projects; dozens of BAU escalations. Per the theme of my last update, there is no chance of a quiet month.

Lastly, but importantly, we’ve also started a communication exercise with C4 leadership teams to ensure everyone understands what we’re building and how it affects them.

For Newton to reach maturity, we need to do a better job helping all our app delivery colleagues and vendor teams understand the standards and processes that they need to follow. It’s hard not to list these out as there are so many elements that have to be considered and we’ve already put a huge effort into improving them:

  • Office Hours & Changelog, to help teams talk to us and Q&A (contact Joe Stanziano)
  • A new, simpler, architecture
  • Revised documentation site
  • Updated policies and standards
  • Automated checks for common mistakes
  • And we’re actively working or planning for:
  • Clarified Governance workflows
  • Process diagrams for every process affecting a Mobile app in C4
  • Revised test standards and frameworks (in partnership with the Test Guild)
  • Metrics and Telemetry, exposed via Dashboard
  • Updated SDKs to make working with Newton Connect simpler
  • Newton Jetpack libraries for component reuse / modularised apps

It’s likely that Tom and I will be coming to talk to you about all the above soon, but keep an eye out for more blog posts and, soon, changelog updates!
