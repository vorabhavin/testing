What's New in iOS 14

by Joseph Stanziano, Manager & Mobile Developer (iOS)

Published: Sept 14, 2020

Newton Mobile’s take on the upcoming changes to iOS

Every year in June, Apple hosts their Worldwide Developer Conference (WWDC.) The event begins with a Keynote where we learn about major changes and the upcoming versions of Apple’s operating systems. The rest of the weeklong event is packed full of sessions aimed at informing developers of new Apple software changes. Sometimes, there are even new hardware announcements.

This year, things were a little different because of the Covid-19 Pandemic. For WWDC2020, Apple’s 31st, the event was 100% remote, free and streamed online to millions of developers and industry peers, live from their headquarters at Apple Park, California.

While the event lacked stage presence, it made up through an impressive range of operating system updates from across Apple’s platform, including iOS 14 - the most significant iPhone update in years. With dozens of updates and new features, there’s a lot to be excited about, so let’s look at the main iOS 14 features of interest.

Widgets

Test Flight App Logo

Widgets have been redesigned in iOS 14 and can now be added directly to a user’s home screen, allowing the iPhone to display relevant and personalized information at a glance.

There are two standout features. The first feature surfaces sensitive information such as events or tasks directly onto the home screen, enabling the user to quickly see if, for example, they have a doctors appointment that day.

The second feature offers a shortcut into specific sections of your app. This is an excellent way to help users carry out repeatable actions faster, such as logging their mood.

And because widgets are updated throughout the day - providing access to key features directly on their home screen - the user always has access to the most relevant information, potentially increasing their engagement.

App Clips

iOS screen showing App Clips

App Clips is another handy feature, enabling users to instantly start using essential parts of your app without searching the App Store or downloading the full app. This can increase the discoverability of our apps and make them and available right when our user needs it.

For example, promotional materials can have App Clips added to them, or, if there is a physical location associated with an app, Siri can suggest the App Clip to someone at that location. Existing users can share App Clips to their friends and family, which provides a great opening experience to the app.

Getting started is simple: Locate App Clips through NFC tags, QR codes, or Apple-designed App Clip codes. They can also be launched and shared from Safari, Maps, and Messages.

HealthKit

iOS screen showing HealthKit

HealthKit received a massive update this year with three brand new features which will help your apps provide even greater value to users.

The first feature allows users to track over thirty different symptoms in HealthKit: Now, along with other measurable metrics such as steps and weight, our apps could allow users log symptoms such as fever, sore throat, or headaches alongside other Health data.

The next feature is that electrocardiogram (ECG) results such as atrial fibrillation or sinus rhythm samples recorded from an Apple Watch are now available to be accessed by third-party apps.

Lastly, Apple has added support for mobility types, including walking speed, step length, stair ascent and descent, and a six-minute walking test.

With these new features, Apple is showing its continued effort to make HealthKit their central repository for health and fitness data. n turn, Pfizer’s apps can provide immense value to our patients by tapping into and building on these critical features.

Privacy

iOS screen showing privacy feature

iOS 14 is giving users more control over their privacy than ever before.

Apple announced a new Privacy Information section on the App Store, which shows the privacy practices of the app before the user downloads it. It’s important to note that we will also now need to provide this information for all of Pfizer’s public iOS apps going forward.

iOS and iPadOS include an identifier that allows app developers to uniquely identify a user and help track app installs and usage between web and app properties. For apps using the Advertising Identifier, iOS 14 users will now receive a prompt requiring opt-in for sharing this information. The impact? It could affect usability flows for apps where we are interested in coordinating analytics and usage data between the web and mobile apps for app install attribution.

FHIRModels

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) is the specification for the exchange of healthcare information electronically. As you can imagine, Health Record data is complicated and extensive. Manually accessing the right information is difficult and time-consuming, especially when you take into account different versions of FHIR. New in iOS 14, Apple has added FHIRModels.

FHIRModels is a native Swift library for FHIR resource data models and contains support for DSTU2, R4, and latest build versions.

FHIRModels handles the model mapping for us, allowing our apps to access critical Health Record data such as medicine dosing instructions, making it much easier for our patients.

Conclusion

So there you have it: standout features for the new iOS 14. Still in public beta, the full release of iOS 14 will roll out later this year, and we’re anticipating more updates and adjustments to smooth out the bugs.

Until then, the Newton Mobile team will continue testing and evaluating iOS 14 over the coming months, and I’m excited to see our apps take advantage of these new features to provide a superior experience and help change patients’ lives.
