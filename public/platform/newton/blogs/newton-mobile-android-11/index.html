Android 11: Optimizing and Personalizing the User Experience

Android 11: Optimizing and Personalizing the User Experience

by Vineetha Venugopal, Newton Mobile Software Engineer

Published: Nov 30, 2021

Apple’s adoption of clearer data usage labels on apps may change the process of submitting a Newton-built app to the App Store, but we’re fully behind the push for a privacy-first customer experience.

Android 11, the newest version of the Android mobile OS, was released in September 2020. Initially available on select Pixel, OnePlus, Xiaomi, OPPO and realme devices, more partners are expected to launch and upgrade devices over the coming months and into 2021. Positioning itself as an all-in-one tool that puts the user front and center, Android 11 includes powerful device controls, streamlines conversation management, and ensures the highest possible level of data privacy. In this article, we take a look at some of the exciting new features in Android 11, how they can make users’ lives easier, and the implications for Pfizer’s Newton Mobile-based apps.

Staying Connected
​​​​​​​

Android widgets

COVID-19 has changed how the world interacts and communicates. Messaging has become ever more critical in helping people to stay connected, and there are any number of messaging apps and channels available. Android 11’s “Conversations and Bubbles” feature centralizes conversations from multiple messaging apps, ensuring that users never miss out on important news, or vital gossip! Key contacts can be tagged so that their messages appear even on the lock screen.

The notification panel for chats displays each person’s avatar combined with the messaging icon, so it’s easy to differentiate between exchanges. A user can create a chat bubble with a single tap, to have handy access to an active conversation while multitasking.

Many Pfizer apps use chat features to facilitate interaction between patients and medical professionals (for example, LivingWith and Pfizer Medical Online). Android’s Bubble API means no more complex code for Pfizer apps to support bubble messaging, and a bye-bye to system alert windows. Implementation is simplified, and the user gets a native experience. For more information, check out the Android documentation here.
​​​​​​​

Controlled devices on Android

Managing Smart Devices

In Android 11, a long press of the power button presents Quick Access Device Controls: a unified view of the controls for all of a user’s gadgets. This turns an Android phone into a must-have control center for smart homes, and a critical tool for patients who depend on connected devices to manage their health and wellbeing.

Device controls are displayed as templated widgets, and are easily implemented by making use of the ControlsProviderService class. This feature opens up opportunities for Pfizer to innovate in the area of connected medical tech. More information is available on the “Device Control” section of Android’s docs.

Maximizing Privacy

In a connected world, we all need to be more vigilant about protecting our data, and especially data related to customers and patients. Android 11 allows cautious users to grant one-time, temporary access when an app requests permissions (for example, location, camera or microphone access). Additionally, in the case of apps that haven’t been used for a long time, permissions are reset after notifying the user.

​​​​​​​

Location permissions on Android

This is something to be cognizant of when designing the user experience. Users may choose not to give continued access to their data if they’re unclear of the benefits, or whether they can trust the app requesting it. Provide clear information to users on what permissions are being sought and why; let them know what you’re going to do with the access, then follow through on your promises. Apps shouldn’t request irrelevant, just-in-case permissions that may make users mistrustful. Be aware of regional and domestic requirements and restrictions when it comes to accessing and using user data, for example, GDPR obligations in the EU.

Most Pfizer apps make use of location data to pinpoint a user’s current location, or to provide information on locations of health centers or rehab centers. Developers of any apps that use background location access, interact with other installed apps, or require camera/microphone/location access should read Android’s documentation on what has changed, and the recommended mitigation strategy.

Native Screen Recording

Android 11’s built-in screen recording means we can wave goodbye to third-party apps, which could pose a privacy or security threat. Pfizer’s app users can now capture what is happening on their phone, along with voice commentary (via mic or phone). It’s then simple to share these recordings when requesting support or logging an issue. For Pfizer’s Android developers, this makes it easier to solicit information and evidence related to device-specific issues.

Accessibility Enhancements

Other Android 11 enhancements that may benefit Pfizer app developers working on accessible healthcare solutions include:

  • Faster and easier voice access
  • Braille typing support
  • The Lookout feature, which can scan labels or documents for people with low vision or blindness

Comprehensive details on these and other features can be found in the Android 11 developer documentation.

Moving Forward

Android 11 is truly user-centric: it includes many new features that put users in control of their experience. Android 11 brings us at Pfizer opportunities to positively enhance our apps, and our users’ experiences, by leveraging this new and improved functionality.

Start migrating your apps now to be ready for the new and updated phones that are on their way, and find out more about the new capabilities in Android 11 that could bring your apps to the next level.
