Newton Platform: A new vision

Newton Platform: A new vision

by Tom DeMaio, Newton Product Owner

Published: Feb 16, 2021

When a widely used build automation tool caused cascading problems at the end of 2020, we used our learnings in automation and platform-wide migration for a smooth transition to a better third-party alternative..

What’s changed?

Newton and Newton Mobile now come under the umbrella name of Newton Platform, and platform sub-components have new names too, linking them explicitly to Newton. These names better reflect how the platform has developed in the last 12 months, and where we see it going from here. This article provides some context on what Newton looks like now, and lets you know what new names replace the old familiar ones.

Some background

Historically, Newton has been the back-end platform for Pfizer digital companion apps like Quitter’s Circle, EmotionSpace and LivingWith. The Newton back-end not only supports these important mobile apps, though: it also supports non-mobile front-end interfaces (like the web), and can also integrate with new UIs like voice assistants and other connected devices. Building on the Newton back-end platform, Newton-Hybrid was an initial iteration of using hybrid app technology (Ionic Framework and Cordova) used to create mobile multi-platform apps (iOS/Android) from a single codebase using web technologies.

In early 2020, Newton Platform DevOps started work to consolidate various mobile-related stacks and teams to a comprehensive mobile platform. This platform was initially named Newton Mobile, in contrast to and separate from Newton MBaaS.

During the course of 2020, as Newton Mobile evolved, we learned many things about the needs of our internal clients and stakeholders. It became clear that a refinement and simplification of our branding would help our clients better understand our product offerings and their value. Building on experience and learning, our updated platform vision better addresses our clients’ needs.

Newton now

Newton is Pfizer Digital’s solution to accelerate and simplify development and delivery of mobile applications to patients, customers, healthcare providers and colleagues across mobile and connected health ecosystems. The Newton Platform covers everything related to mobile apps managed by the Pfizer Digital Customer & Commercial Creation Center Platform DevOps team. At Pfizer, Newton is to mobile as Edison is to the web.

The Newton Platform includes everything from source code, utilities, continuous integration, release management, app store management, metrics, self-service capabilities, template code, reusable code components, mobile integration APIs, authentication services, push notifications and anything else managed at the platform level and related to mobile application development.

The platform is divided into three product lines:

  • Newton Pipeline is the new name for Newton CI/CD. It covers application lifecycle, compliance and release management. It includes Newton Dashboard, Newton AppCheck, and Newton BetaApps.

  • Newton Jetpack encapsulates everything that used to make up Newton Mobile App Framework and Newton Mobile Lite. It provides application templates, reusable code and components, and UI elements. It is made up of Newton Jetpack UI, Newton SDK, and Newton Template.

  • Newton Connect is the new name for the Newton back end platform. This has been conceptually broadened to consist of the capabilities related to: identity management and authentication services; content delivery; data sync and storage; push notifications; and other integrations used by mobile apps and websites. It is also the conduit through which mobile apps can integrate to deliver value to users from our other Pfizer platforms: Edison, Stratus, and Marketing Automation. Its components include Newton API, Newton Auth, Newton Admin UI, Newton Push, and Newton Edison Connector.
