'I’m amazed at what's being achieved': Newton/Marketing Automation monthly roundup 

Newton/Marketing Automation monthly roundup

by Hugh Fidgen, Newton & Rosalind Platform Lead

Published: Mar 2, 2021


Being asked to write a roundup of “what’s going on at the moment” has been really tricky. Not because of writer’s block or lack of material, but because so much is changing.
​​​​​​​
Change is a constant, so the saying goes, and Pfizer’s pace of change can be unrelenting at times, but this year feels different, for obvious reasons.

Even if COVID-19 has been somewhat normalized now, nothing is normal. All our lives have been turned upside down by this virus and even if we’re lucky enough to be safe and well, the mental drain of lockdowns or the lack of social interaction has a huge impact.

Pfizer is also in the midst of significant strategic changes. These were started before the pandemic but in many cases the pandemic has accelerated them as the entire organization turns towards Digital to boost productivity and deliver new ways of engaging with Healthcare Profesionals (HCPs) and Patients. The recent blog series about the COVID-19 website launches illustrate why and the business demand for our capabilities is surging as Bold Moves gain traction and investments turn into product/project development.

Being in the right place (Pfizer) at the right time (Now) to help deliver such foundational change is very exciting, but it can also be exhausting if you let the demands get on top of you, so I hope everyone is looking after themselves, taking time out and actively managing their pandemic lives!

So, this is the backdrop against which “what’s going on” plays out and suffice to say I’m absolutely amazed at what is being achieved by the Newton and Marketing Automation teams.

First, you’ve hopefully seen (via our new Newton branding) that we’re moving fast on three interconnected Mobile fronts:

  • Newton Pipeline – the fundamental CI and associated automation that gets code from GitHub all the way to app stores.

  • Newton Jetpack – the developer experience, documentation and reusable SDKs and component packs that get teams off the ground faster.

  • Newton Connect – the back-end infrastructure, APIs and integrations that power our apps and connect them to a global vendor ecosystem of capabilities.

What you won’t have seen is the effort behind the scenes going in to get rid of technical debt (more to come on this in the near future) and weave all this capability, automation and process optimisation into a modern platform that is more easily maintainable and makes a significant difference to teams trying to develop apps.

One example of such a “back office” change is that Newton Connect is now being developed by our Marketing Automation teammates. This makes sense when you realize that Newton Connect and MAP/PMAP share the same underlying technologies and that by breaking silos and pooling teams we can be even more responsive in management of our applications.

We’re making all these changes because Pfizer needs modern, integrated, Mobile platform capabilities in order to meet the demands of the Digital Companion launches being scheduled for the next few years.

We’re working closely with the Product Owner, Client Partners and Patient Tower to ensure that our roadmaps align and that we’re delivering the capabilities they need.

One such initiative being delivered now by our newly-merged Newton Connect team is the creation of Newton Connect PHI (Protected Health Information), a sister offering to Newton Connect that is designed from the ground up to manage the legal and regulatory landscape required if we are to host the sensitive personal data that the next generation of Digital Companions require.

On the Marketing Automation side of the team, we are also about to announce some branding changes that reflect our new realities and Pfizer’s new strategies. It’ll of course be named for a scientist, and with a great reason for the name choice.

Beyond the daily work of keeping the Marketing Automation platforms running, developing enhancements and delivering Newton Connect, the team are in the midst of Proof of Concepts (PoCs) and plans for how we implement new Journey Orchestrations capabilities.

These will bring personalized content, A/B testing and more to NextGen, Edison and Newton. A Journey Orchestration tool will revolutionize how we understand and interact with our HCPs and Patients, enabling us to completely overhaul our architectures and capabilities for personalized Marketing. The Bold Move 3.3 HCP and Patient Engagement Strategies are actively looking at this, so it seems likely we shall see “MAP 2.0” and other such programs in the coming years.

So… there you go, just a few of the things happening in my space, as we all sit in our spare bedrooms and kitchens, spread out around the world this crazy year.

Thank you to everyone on my team for knocking it out the park so consistently, but also to everyone in Customer and Commercial Creation Center (C&CCC) for the positive attitude, messaging and culture that makes it possible to survive or even thrive right now.

Please feel free to ping me on Teams if you want to know more about anything I’ve mentioned today. There will definitely be blog posts on the details of each change and some of the behind-the-scenes activities in the near future.

Stay safe!

Hugh
